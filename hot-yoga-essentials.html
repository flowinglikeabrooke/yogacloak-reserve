hot yoga essentials

For the heat, the sweat, and the leaving.

Most hot yoga lists stop at the mat. The part after class has its own needs: skin still warm, clothes still damp, and a day that keeps moving.

A towel that can keep up.

For hot yoga, a towel is not optional. You want something that absorbs sweat without bunching, slipping, or turning heavy halfway through practice.

Water, more than you think.

Bring water in, and keep drinking after. Heat classes can make the walk out feel longer than the class itself, especially when your body is still cooling down.

Clothes that dry quietly.

Breathable, sweat-wicking pieces help during class. After class, the real question becomes what sits over damp clothes without feeling fussy or exposed.

An after-class layer.

A cover-up or wrap can do what a spare sweatshirt often cannot: absorb a little, breathe a little, and still look intentional from studio to street.

That is where The Cloak and The Wrap sit - between a yoga towel, a robe, and the thing you actually want to leave in.

after the heat

The room was 105°. Then you walked outside.

Hot yoga ends, but your body doesn't switch back right away. Here's what's actually happening, and why it gets cold so fast.

Sweat is supposed to cool you down.

That's the whole point of sweating. Water evaporates off your skin and pulls heat with it. It works exactly as designed, right up until you stop moving and walk into a parking lot or a cold studio hallway.

Once you stop, the cooling doesn't stop.

Evaporation keeps happening whether you need it or not. Damp clothes against skin, plus moving air, is a fast way to lose heat, which is why the ten minutes after class can feel colder than the ten minutes before it.

A dry layer isn't always enough.

Throwing a sweatshirt on over damp clothes just traps the moisture closer to your skin. It buys time, not warmth. What actually helps is something that can absorb the dampness itself, not just sit on top of it.

That's the gap yogacloak fills.

The Cloak and The Wrap are built for exactly this window: the few minutes between the heat of class and getting back to a normal temperature, when damp skin meets cooler air.

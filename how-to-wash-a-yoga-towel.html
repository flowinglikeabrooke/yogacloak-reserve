care guide

Wash it right, and it keeps up.

Absorbent fabric only stays absorbent if you wash it the right way. The short version: cold water, no fabric softener, and a little patience with the dryer.

Cold water, every time.

Heat is harder on technical fabric than people expect. It can break down the fibers that make a towel or wrap absorbent in the first place. Wash cold, and wash with like colors so nothing bleeds onto it.

Skip the fabric softener.

Fabric softener works by coating fibers in a thin layer of residue. That residue is what makes regular laundry feel soft, but on anything built to absorb sweat, it does the opposite: it seals the fabric and kills the absorbency you bought it for.

Low heat, or just hang it.

Tumble dry on low if you're in a hurry. Hanging it to air dry is gentler and helps it hold its shape over time, especially for heavier pieces.

It actually improves with washing.

Technical fabric like this tends to soften with every wash instead of breaking down. The Cloak and The Wrap are built to take repeat washing. That's part of the point.

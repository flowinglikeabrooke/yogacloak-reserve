after hot yoga

Wear something that understands the in-between.

After hot yoga, the outfit problem is specific: your skin is warm, your clothes are damp, and the outside world is not designed for that feeling.

Start with breathable base layers.

Sweat-wicking leggings, shorts, and tanks help during class. They should move, dry, and stay close without holding too much heat after.

Skip the heavy sweatshirt.

A thick cotton layer can feel comforting for a minute, then damp and bulky. After heat, you want coverage without the trapped weight.

Look for absorbent coverage.

A post-class layer should handle moisture, cover the body, and still feel quiet enough for coffee, errands, or the ride home.

The yoga towel problem, but wearable.

A towel solves sweat. Apparel solves being seen. yogacloak is built in that overlap: absorbent like a useful textile, shaped like something you can leave in.
